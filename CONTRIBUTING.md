# Contributing to CHLOYA

CHLOYA is developed chapter by chapter. Feedback, proposals, and criticism are accepted for published methodology chapters.

## What you can contribute

- clarifications of terms and definitions;
- reports of logical contradictions or unclear wording;
- links to research, standards, official documentation, and practical cases;
- threat models, prompt-injection scenarios, and failure analyses;
- pilot-validation results, including negative results;
- proposals for project-memory structures, templates, and examples;
- examples of adapting CHLOYA to existing repositories;
- translation proposals after a chapter's Russian version has been approved.

## Which chapters are open for feedback

Feedback is accepted for chapters published in the main [documentation table of contents](docs/README.md): their titles are links. Chapters without links are still in development and do not yet accept public text feedback.

## How to submit feedback

Include:

1. the chapter link and version;
2. the relevant section or passage;
3. the type of feedback: error, ambiguity, contradiction, proposal, source, or practical experience;
4. rationale and, where possible, a verifiable source or example;
5. suggested wording, if available.

Use [GitHub Issues](../../issues) for specific text feedback so that discussion remains tied to the methodology version and chapter.

## Translations into other languages

Translations of published CHLOYA chapters into other languages are welcome and may be contributed freely.

A translation may be based on an available published Russian or English version, at the translator’s discretion. It must identify the source chapter, source language, and source version.

The translator is responsible for translation accuracy and for preserving meaning, structure, section numbering, and source numbers. Bibliography titles remain in their original language.

The project author currently maintains only the Russian and English versions of the methodology. Translations into other languages are maintained by their translators and the community; they may lag behind the current Russian or English version.

Before starting a substantial translation, contributors are encouraged to open an Issue or contact the author to agree on the structure and avoid parallel work on the same text.

## How decisions are made

The author considers feedback but is not obliged to accept every proposal. Substantive accepted changes are recorded in the iteration's [change history](docs/en/CHANGELOG.md).

## Sources, licenses, and confidentiality

New sources must include a verifiable link and access date. Source titles in the bibliography remain in their original language.
